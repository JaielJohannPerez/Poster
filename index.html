Conclusions

The study concludes that the floor vacuuming robot is structured around various components such as structural, mechanical, control, sensing, and power supply. Its design focuses on integrating these components efficiently, with specifications like integrating an expansion board to the Arduino UNO R3. Operationally, it uses Pulse Width Modulation for speed regulation, IR and ultrasonic sensors for obstacle detection, and a high-speed fan for suction, activated by a battery connection switch. In terms of performance, the robot detects objects up to 200 cm away, completes tasks in 10 minutes on a 25 sqm surface, and consumes approximately 1800 mA, allowing for around 2 hours and 30 minutes of operation on two LiPo batteries with a capacity of 4600 mAH.

Study Findings

The study on the floor vacuuming robot revealed the following key findings:

  1. Structure and Design: The robot consists of structural, mechanical, control, sensing, and power supply components, with detailed specifications regarding its design and integration of components.
  2. Operational Aspects:
    • Speed: Controlled through Pulse Width Modulation, the robot's speed ranges from 0mm/s to 500mm/s.
    • Obstacle Handling: Utilizes IR and ultrasonic sensors to detect obstacles and maneuver accordingly.
    • Vacuum Integration: Incorporates a 12V fan and filter for effective suction and dust collection.
  3. Performance:
    • Navigation Efficiency: Accurately identifies obstacles and completes cleaning tasks efficiently.
    • Cleaning Capabilities: Effectively removes dust, sand, flour, and small debris, with limitations on larger paper pieces.
    • Power Consumption: Operates for approximately 2.5 hours on two LiPo batteries, consuming around 1800 mA.
Overall, the robot demonstrates efficient navigation, cleaning capabilities, and moderate power consumption, with room for improvement in handling larger debris.

Introduction

Meet our battery-powered, Arduino-based floor vacuuming robot, designed for the College of Teacher Education Building hallways at Batangas State University. Despite budget constraints and design challenges, our robot excels in speed, navigation, and object identification.

This innovative project showcases our dedication to creating efficient and cost-effective solutions. Our robot is not just a simple vacuum cleaner; it's a smart device capable of navigating complex environments and identifying obstacles with precision. The Arduino platform provides a robust foundation, allowing us to implement sophisticated algorithms that enhance the robot's performance.

We faced numerous hurdles during the development process, including limited funding and technical obstacles. However, our team remained committed and resourceful, finding creative ways to overcome these challenges. The result is a highly capable robot that operates efficiently in real-world settings.

Our vacuuming robot is a testament to what can be achieved with ingenuity and determination. We invite you to explore our project, learn more about its features, and see how it can contribute to a cleaner and smarter educational environment at Batangas State University.
